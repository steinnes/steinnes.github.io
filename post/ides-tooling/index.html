Tooling vs. IDEs

These days I once in a while need to use XCode. I probably should use it more often, and do a lot more work on our iOS codebase. But I use it rarely and the fact remains that the IDE frustrates me to no end every time, and it’s not just Xcode.

Why do I not like IDE’s?

Let’s take a step back and think about the purpose of an IDE, surely they have a noble purpose, right? To automate fiddly repetitive tasks which the developer takes no pleasure in, and might be error prone as well, right? It can give you great interfaces for detecting errors as you type, suggesting names, fixes, run tests, integrate with production and deployments even. I mean, what’s not to like?
